# Voice AI for Telephone Reception in SMEs

The telephone remains the preferred communication channel for many Swiss SMEs, especially in the service, healthcare, real estate, and craft sectors. However, managing phone calls presents a recurring challenge: missed calls outside business hours, frustrating wait times, overwhelmed receptionists, and lost prospects who hang up without leaving a message.

Voice artificial intelligence provides a concrete solution to these issues, with systems capable of answering calls 24/7, qualifying requests, scheduling appointments, and transferring urgent calls—all in natural and fluent Swiss French.

## The Problem of Missed Calls

### An Invisible but Real Cost

Studies conducted in the Swiss market reveal concerning figures:

- **38% of incoming calls** to Swiss SMEs go unanswered
- A missed call from a prospect represents an average of **CHF 250 to 500** in potential lost revenue
- **67% of callers** who reach voicemail do not leave a message
- **82% of customers** believe that the quality of telephone reception influences their perception of the company

For an SME receiving 30 calls per day, these figures translate to 11 missed calls daily and a potential loss of CHF 2,750 to 5,500 per day.

### Traditional Solutions and Their Limitations

| Solution | Monthly Cost (CHF) | Availability | Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full-time receptionist | 5,500 – 7,000 | 8am-5pm, Mon-Fri | Excellent but limited |
| External secretary service | 800 – 2,500 | Business hours | Variable |
| Automated system (IVR) | 50 – 200 | 24/7 | Poor (rigid menu trees) |
| Voicemail | 0 | 24/7 | Very low |

None of these solutions combine permanent availability, high interaction quality, and controlled costs. This is precisely where voice AI excels.

## What Is a Voice AI Assistant?

### Technical Functionality

A modern voice AI assistant combines several technologies:

- **Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR)**: real-time transcription of speech into text
- **Natural Language Understanding (NLU)**: analysis of the caller's intent
- **Large Language Model (LLM)**: generation of contextual and natural responses
- **Text-to-Speech (TTS)**: delivery of responses in a natural voice
- **Dialogue Management**: tracking conversation flow and handling interruptions

### What a Voice AI Assistant Can Do

- Answer calls instantly, 24/7
- Identify the purpose of the call (information request, appointment scheduling, complaint, emergency)
- Answer frequently asked questions (hours, prices, location, services)
- Schedule appointments by consulting the calendar in real time
- Qualify prospects (budget, needs, urgency) and transfer qualified calls
- Take structured messages and send them via email or to the CRM
- Handle calls in French, German, English, and Italian
- Transfer to a human when necessary

### What a Voice AI Assistant Cannot Do (Yet)

- Handle emotionally complex situations (conflicts, serious complaints)
- Understand very strong accents or very noisy environments
- Fully replace human interaction for high-value commercial relationships

## Implementation: From Idea to Deployment

### Phase 1: Call Flow Analysis (1 Week)

Before deploying a voice assistant, analyze your call flows:

- How many calls do you receive daily/weekly?
- What is the breakdown by type (information, appointments, complaints, sales)?
- When are calls most frequent?
- How many calls are missed and why?
- What questions are most frequently asked?

### Phase 2: Assistant Configuration (2-4 Weeks)

- Define the conversational script (scenarios, responses, escalations)
- Record or select the voice
- Integrate with the calendar and [CRM](/fr/blog/crm-intelligent-ia)
- Set up transfer rules (when to escalate to a human)
- Configure notifications and reports

### Phase 3: Testing and Adjustment (2 Weeks)

- Internal testing with varied scenarios
- Pilot phase with a limited number of lines
- Collect feedback and adjust the script
- Optimize understanding for specific cases

### Phase 4: Gradual Deployment (Ongoing)

- Activate on all lines
- Daily monitoring during the first two weeks
- Monthly conversation analysis and continuous improvement
- Expand to new use cases (outbound calls, satisfaction surveys)

## Compliance and Ethics

### Legal Obligations in Switzerland

- **Inform the caller**: the person must know they are interacting with an automated assistant
- **Offer human transfer**: the caller must be able to request to speak to a person at any time
- **Call recording**: if conversations are recorded, consent is required
- **Data protection**: information collected during the call is subject to the [nLPD](/fr/blog/nlpd-ia-obligations-pme)

### Best Practices

- The assistant clearly introduces itself as a virtual assistant
- A human transfer is always offered
- Call data is stored in compliance with the nLPD
- Recordings are deleted after the legal retention period
