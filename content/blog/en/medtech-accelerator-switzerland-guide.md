# Medtech Accelerator Switzerland: Programs & Criteria

**Short answer:** the strongest medtech accelerator in Switzerland depends on your stage. DayOne Basel is strong for digital health and clinical access, Switzerland Innovation Park supports hardware and manufacturing, Venture Kick helps with investor readiness, and Innosuisse supports R&D with academic partners.

Switzerland is one of Europe's most advanced medtech ecosystems, with deep expertise in precision manufacturing, diagnostics, robotics, clinical research and regulated product development. For medtech startups and SMEs, the challenge is not only finding a program. It is choosing the right path for product maturity, clinical evidence, Swissmedic expectations, EU MDR alignment and AI validation.

## Switzerland's Medtech Ecosystem at a Glance

### Key clusters

**Jura Arc (Watchtech → Medtech)**: The Jura region's precision manufacturing heritage has translated directly into world-class medtech manufacturing. Companies like Straumann (dental), Stryker Switzerland, and Dentsply Sirona have roots here.

**Basel Life Sciences**: The pharma/biotech cluster around Basel supports a strong medtech segment, especially in diagnostics (Roche Diagnostics) and imaging.

**Zurich MedTech**: University Hospital Zurich, ETH Zurich robotics labs, and a growing cluster of surgical robotics startups.

**Romandy**: EPFL spin-offs in medical robotics, implantable electronics, and smart medical devices.

### Regulatory context

Swiss medtech operates under:
- **Swissmedic**: Swiss regulatory authority for medical devices (aligned with EU MDR/IVDR since 2021)
- **EU MDR 2017/745**: products need EU MDR CE marking for EU market access
- **IVD Regulation**: specific rules for in-vitro diagnostics
- **FADP/nLPD**: Swiss data protection for patient data in connected devices

This regulatory environment is complex but well-supported by specialist consultants and accelerator programs.

## Top Medtech Accelerators in Switzerland

### 1. DayOne Basel — The Leading Medtech Accelerator

[DayOne](https://www.dayone.swiss) is Switzerland's premier accelerator at the intersection of medtech, biotech, and digital health. Based in Basel, it provides unmatched access to the Swiss pharma cluster.

**Program details:**
- **Duration**: 6 months (2 cohorts/year)
- **Sectors**: medical devices, diagnostics, digital therapeutics, surgical tools, implantables, connected health
- **Corporate partners**: Novartis, Roche, Lonza, Bayer, B. Braun, Straumann
- **Hospital access**: University Hospital Basel, Kantonsspital Aarau
- **Regulatory support**: dedicated Swissmedic/EU MDR pathway advisory

**What sets DayOne apart for medtech:**
- Access to GMP-compliant prototyping labs in Basel
- Direct introductions to procurement teams at university hospitals for pilot negotiations
- Biobanking and clinical dataset access for AI validation
- Connection to EU Notified Bodies for CE marking process

### 2. Switzerland Innovation Park — Jura/Basel/Bern

The Switzerland Innovation network provides infrastructure specifically designed for hardware-intensive medtech development:

**Jura Innovation Park** (Le Noirmont): precision manufacturing labs, cleanrooms, MEMS prototyping, microtechnology for implantable devices.

**Basel Innovation Park**: Life sciences labs, analytical chemistry, bioprocess development.

**Bern Innovation Park**: defense tech and aerospace manufacturing technology relevant to surgical robotics and imaging systems.

### 3. Venture Kick — Medtech track

[Venture Kick](/fr/blog/venture-kick-programme-suisse) has supported several Swiss medtech companies with staged non-dilutive support and access to a strong investor network. Medtech startups with a clear IP strategy and a TRL 4+ prototype consistently perform well in Phase 2 and 3.

**Medtech-specific evaluation criteria at Venture Kick:**
- IP/patent strategy (filed or filing in progress)
- Regulatory pathway clarity (predicate device, MDR class)
- Clinical evidence plan (what studies are needed for CE marking)
- Reimbursement strategy (Swiss DRG, EU payer access)

### 4. Innosuisse — Medtech R&D Programs

InnoSuisse co-funds medtech R&D through its standard instruments with specific medtech academic partners:

- **Innovation checks**: feasibility with HES-SO Health, HEIG-VD, ZHAW Life Sciences, ETH Zurich or other academic partners
- **R&D projects**: collaborative development with academic partners in biomedical engineering, materials science or medical AI
- **BRIDGE**: technology transfer from academic labs to medical companies

Key academic partners for medtech:
- **ETH Zurich Biomedical Engineering**: surgical robotics, implantables, imaging
- **EPFL Center for Neuroprosthetics**: neural interfaces, implantable electronics
- **ZHAW Life Sciences** (Wädenswil/Zurich): diagnostics, bioprocess, analytical methods
- **HE Arc** (Neuchâtel/Biel): precision engineering, microfluidics, watchtech-medtech crossover

### 5. The Ark — Valais Medtech

[The Ark](https://www.theark.ch) in Sion (Valais) has a medtech cluster connected to the Idiap Research Institute and HES-SO Valais.

- Focus: biosensors, AI-assisted diagnostics, telemedicine
- Funding: direct The Ark fund investment (minority stake possible)
- Academic partner: IDIAP (signal processing, medical AI)

AI is being applied to the regulatory process itself:

### Connected devices and remote monitoring

Swiss medtech companies are developing AI-powered connected devices:

- **Implantable sensors**: AI algorithms processing sensor data for disease management (cardiac, orthopedic, neurological)
- **Home monitoring**: AI triage of remote patient data to trigger clinical escalation only when needed

## How to choose the right medtech accelerator in Switzerland

Use the stage of your project as the filter:

| Stage | Best-fit support | Main question |
|---|---|---|
| Idea or lab prototype | Academic transfer and feasibility programs | Is the clinical need real and testable? |
| Prototype with early evidence | Venture Kick, Innosuisse, regional innovation parks | Can the device be validated and protected? |
| Clinical pilot ready | DayOne Basel, hospital partnerships, specialist mentors | Can the product survive clinical and regulatory scrutiny? |
| Manufacturing preparation | Switzerland Innovation Park, Jura Arc, ISO specialists | Can quality, traceability and production scale? |
| Market entry | Regulatory, reimbursement and commercial partners | Can clinicians, hospitals and buyers adopt it? |

A strong application normally includes: clinical need, target users, technical maturity, IP position, regulatory class, evidence plan, reimbursement thinking and a realistic route to market.

## FAQ — Medtech Accelerator Switzerland

**What is the typical timeline from Swiss medtech accelerator to CE marking?**
The accelerator phase typically adds 6–18 months of validated clinical evidence and regulatory preparation. From DayOne participation to CE marking, realistic timelines are: Class I devices: 18–24 months; Class IIa: 3–4 years; Class IIb/III: 5–7 years. AI components add regulatory review time but often compress clinical evidence collection.

**Does a Swiss medtech startup need to comply with both Swiss and EU MDR?**
Yes. Switzerland's MepV (Medizinprodukteverordnung) is aligned with EU MDR 2017/745. CE marking is required for EU market access, and Swissmedic accepts CE-marked products for Swiss market entry. Practically, getting EU CE marking first covers both markets.

**How does AI change the regulatory pathway for medtech in Switzerland?**
AI/ML-based software as a medical device (SaMD) is regulated under EU MDR Article 2 (software) and Swissmedic's aligned guidance. Key requirements: transparency, explainability, performance monitoring post-market, and change management protocols when the AI model is updated. The IMDRF AI/ML SaMD guidance document (adopted by Swissmedic) provides the framework.

**Can Swiss medtech startups access EIC Accelerator (Horizon Europe) funding?**
Switzerland has partial association to Horizon Europe in 2026 (negotiations ongoing). EIC Accelerator support can be relevant for breakthrough medtech innovations. Check the current status with Innosuisse or Europe Enterprise Network Switzerland.

**What makes a medtech startup application to DayOne stronger?**
DayOne jury members (pharma executives, hospital CMOs, medtech investors) prioritize: (1) clear unmet clinical need with quantified patient impact; (2) evidence of clinical partnerships (letters of support from physicians/hospitals); (3) realistic regulatory pathway with timeline; (4) team with combined clinical, engineering, and commercial expertise; (5) credible IP strategy.
